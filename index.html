NEUTROPHIL

Neutrophils are small round cells with a diameter of approx. 9-15μm. The nucleus is multi-lobulated (usu. made up of 2-5 lobes) while the cytoplasm contains fine granules. It is the most abundant type of WBC in a normal peripheral blood film.

LYMPHOCYTE

Lymphocytes are small round cells with a large, intensely stained nucleus and scanty cytoplasm, resulting in a high nuclear to cytoplasmic (N/C) ratio. It is the second most abundant type of WBC in a normal peripheral blood film.

MONOCYTE

Monocyte is the largest type of normal WBC, ranging approx. from 15-25μm in diameter, and contains abundant cytoplasm. The nucleus is often lobulated/indented, causing a bean-shaped or kidney-shaped appearance.

EOSINOPHIL

Eosinophils are spherical in shape and about 15μm in diameter. The nucleus is usually two-lobed while the cytoplasm contains granules that are stained in orange to red color by acidic dyes.

BASOPHIL

Basophils are spherical in shape and ranging approx. 10-15μm in diameter. The nucleus is usually two-lobed while the cytoplasm contains large round granules that are stained in purple-black color with basic dyes, often obscuring the nucleus.
